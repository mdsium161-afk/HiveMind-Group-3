# HiveMind × Ravensbourne --- Week 1 Pack (Discovery & Hypotheses)

This pack tells you **exactly** how to run your Week 1 research
interviews (30--40 mins), who to interview, what to ask, and what to
submit so your work counts.

Tuesday 24 March (end of day).**

## 1) What you must achieve in Week 1

transcripts and produce a shared summary of the main onboarding +
knowledge pain points.

## 2) Who to interview (recruitment rules)

**Target audience:** people with real workplace experience.

✅ **Good participants:**

\- Anyone with **1+ year** work experience.

\- Ideally (not required): people who joined a new job or changed teams
in the last **1--3 years**.

\- Any industry is fine.

🚫 **Not allowed:** - Other students (their answers won't count).

### Template for quick outreach message (copy/paste)

> Hi! I'm working on a university project about improving workplace
> onboarding and how people find information at work. Could I interview
> you for **30--40 minutes** this week? It's not a sales call, just
> learning from your experience. With your permission, I'll record audio
> so I can produce a transcript. Your answers will be anonymous.

## 3) Evidence rules (how to make an interview "count")

For **every** interview, you must have:

1)  **Audio recording** (phone recorder / Teams / Zoom audio is fine)

2)  **Transcript** (typed text)

3)  Correct **file naming** + saved in the correct folder

### File naming format

Use one consistent naming format so we can collate quickly:

`GroupName_PXX_YYYY-MM-DD_Interview.md`\
**Example:** `Group3_P01_2026-03-21_Interview.md`

### Where to store transcripts in your repo

-   `research/transcripts/`

### Transcript quality (make it unambiguous)

Your transcript must be **good enough that someone who was not in the
interview can understand the conversation** and pull out clear pain
points.

✅ **Minimum requirements (must-have):**

\- **Speaker labels on every turn** (Interviewer / Participant).

\- **Participant ID only** (e.g., P01) - do **not** include names.

\- **Full sentences where possible** (fix obvious auto‑transcription
errors).

✅ **Strongly recommended (helps synthesis):**

\- Add **timestamps every 2--5 minutes** or whenever a key point is
made.

#### Example (GOOD transcript snippet)

``` text
[00:00] Interviewer: Thanks for joining. Can I record this so I can write a transcript?
[00:05] Participant P03: Yes, that's fine.

[06:12] Interviewer: When you need an answer at work, where do you go first?
[06:18] Participant P03: Usually Teams. I search old messages or ask in a channel.
[06:28] Interviewer: And if you don't find it there?
[06:31] Participant P03: Then I check SharePoint, then email. It's annoying because I never know where the latest doc is.
[06:45] Interviewer: What happens when you find two versions?
[06:49] Participant P03: I waste time. Sometimes I ask a colleague just to confirm which one is correct.
```

#### Example (NOT OK --- too unclear)

``` text
P03: Teams... sharepoint... hard. lots of tools.
Interviewer: ok.
P03: yeah.
```

Auto‑transcription is allowed, but you must clean obvious errors so it
makes sense.

## 4) Interview structure (30--40 minutes)

**All groups must use the full question list below** so the cohort's
findings are comparable.

### Timing plan (recommended)

1)  **Intro + consent + 2‑minute demo** --- 5 mins
2)  **Work context + recent onboarding** --- 8 mins
3)  **Finding information / Dashboard** --- 10 mins
4)  **Onboarding / First‑time experience** --- 8 mins
5)  **AI assistant trust** --- 6 mins
6)  **Wrap‑up** --- 3 mins

Total: \~40 mins

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5) Interview scripts - use these as guidelines

### 5.1 Intro + consent (before demo) --- \~2 mins

> Hi, thanks for your time. I'm a Ravensbourne student working with
> HiveMind on improving workplace onboarding and how people find
> information at work.
>
> This is **not a sales call**. We are just learning from your
> experience. With your permission, I'd like to **record** this so I can
> create a transcript. We'll remove your name and keep it anonymous.
>
> The interview will take **30--40 minutes**. You can skip any question
> or stop at any time.
>
> Is it okay if I record?

✅ Only start recording after they say **yes**.

### 5.2 Two‑minute platform demo (very short) --- \~2 mins

**Goal:** give interviewees enough context to understand what HiveMind
is building.

> I'll show a very quick 2‑minute demo of an early draft (it's not
> final). HiveMind has three main features:
>
> **1) Onboarding feature (guided journey):** modular onboarding
> journeys tailored by **role and team**. It gives a guided "what to do
> next" workflow with **micro‑learning** so new starters aren't
> overwhelmed at the beginning.
>
> **2) Knowledge Hub feature (single source of truth):** a clear,
> central place where company information lives (processes, policies,
> tools, "how we do things here"). The goal is that employees can
> **navigate and find key information** needed for their day‑to‑day job.
>
> **3) AI Assistant feature (quick answers):** you can ask a question
> and the assistant answers using the company information in the
> Knowledge Hub. It's for when you don't know where to look, or you
> don't have time to search through lots of documents.
>
> Now I'll ask questions about your real work experience --- what's
> hard, what helps, and what you wish existed.

### 5.3 How to conduct the interview (simple rules)

-   Ask the question, then **pause** (don't fill the silence).
-   Use simple follow‑ups:
    -   "Can you tell me more?"
    -   "What happened next?"
    -   "Why was that difficult?"
    -   "How often does that happen?"
-   Keep it neutral (don't lead the participant).
-   Watch the clock. If they go long:
    -   "That's really helpful --- I'm mindful of time, can I jump to
        the next question?"

# 6) Full question list (ALL groups must ask ALL questions)

Keep language simple. If they don't understand, rephrase.

## Section A --- Your role + company context (2--3 mins)

1.  What is your current job role/title?
    -   If you're between jobs, what was your most recent role?
2.  What industry is your company in (or your most recent company)?
3.  Roughly how big is the company? (choose one)
    -   1--10, 11--50, 51--200, 201--1,000, 1,000+

## Section B --- Work + recent onboarding (6--7 mins)

4.  Think about the last time you started a job (or changed team). What
    did the first days/weeks look like?
5.  In the first two weeks, what were the **top 3 things** you needed to
    know to do your job well?
6.  What was the hardest part of getting started? Why?
7.  Did onboarding feel helpful and relevant, or did it feel like
    **box‑ticking**? What made it feel that way?

## Section C --- Finding information at work (Knowledge Hub / Dashboard topic) (10 mins)

8.  When you need an answer at work, where do you go first? (people,
    email, Teams/Slack, SharePoint, Google Drive, wiki, etc.)
9.  How often do you have to check **more than one place** to find what
    you need? What's annoying about that?
10. Tell me about a time you couldn't find something important. What
    happened?
11. How do you personally keep track of important links/documents/tasks?
    (notes, bookmarks, saved messages, spreadsheets...) What's not great
    about your method?
12. If you had **one home screen** at work showing the most important
    things, what would you want it to show? (tasks, deadlines, key
    links, people to contact, updates, progress)
13. What information should be super quick to reach --- like within **a
    few clicks**?
14. When you find a document or answer, how do you know it's correct or
    up to date?

## Section D --- Starting a job (Smart Onboarding / first‑time experience) (8 mins)

15. On your first day, what would have made you feel less lost?
16. In your first week, did you clearly know what to do next each day?
    If not, what was missing?
17. What makes onboarding feel **personal and useful** (not generic)?
18. What made you feel "I'm doing okay / I'm on track"? (feedback,
    milestones, manager check‑ins, visible progress)
19. When you needed help, did you know **who to ask** and how to get
    help quickly? What got in the way?

## Section E --- AI helper trust (Trustworthy AI Assistant) (6 mins)

20. Have you used AI tools at work (chatbots, AI search, "copilot"
    tools)? What was good or bad about it?
21. If an AI tool gives you an answer at work, what would make you trust
    it? (links to sources, showing where info came from, explaining
    "why")
22. What would make you *not* trust it?
23. What kinds of workplace questions should an AI tool say: "You should
    ask a human"? (HR, sensitive issues, policy decisions, etc.)
24. Would you worry about privacy if an AI tool used internal documents
    or employee data? What would reassure you?

## Section F --- Wrap‑up (3 mins)

25. What were the **top 2 problems** you mentioned today?

26. What do those problems cost you? (time, stress, mistakes, delays)

27. If you could change **one thing** to make starting a job easier,
    what would it be?

# 8) Week 1 synthesis (turn interviews into hypotheses)

Once your group finishes all interviews:

### Step 1 --- Create a pain point list

-   Each teammate writes 5--10 pain points they heard (short bullets).
-   Combine into one list.
-   Group into themes (examples):
    -   "I can't find things quickly."
    -   "Info is scattered across tools."
    -   "Onboarding feels generic / box‑ticking."
    -   "I don't know what to do next."
    -   "I don't trust AI unless it shows sources/why."

### Step 2 --- Write 3--5 hypotheses (required format)

Create `docs/hypotheses.md` and write 3--5 hypotheses using this format:

    H1: We believe that [idea] will help [type of user] because [what we heard in interviews].
    H2: ...
    H3: ...

### Step 3 --- Draft early success measures

Create `docs/success-metrics.md` and draft 3--5 simple measures you can
test later (examples):

-   "Can people find key info in a few clicks?"

-   "Do people understand what the platform does in the first minute?"

-   "Do people trust AI answers more when sources are shown?"
