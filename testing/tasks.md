# Critical User Tasks — Group 3 (Christoph Dashboard)

These are the 3 task scenarios used in both internal dry-run (Week 3) and live usability testing (Week 4). Each task is grounded in a real pain point identified in our Week 1–2 interviews.

---

## Task 1 — Find a Key Document Fast

**Scenario given to participant:**
> "It's your second week at a new company. Your manager has asked you to review the company's data compliance policy before a meeting in 10 minutes. Using this platform, find the policy and confirm it's the most up-to-date version."

**What we are testing:**
- Navigation to the Knowledge Hub from the Dashboard
- Search functionality and document filtering
- Trust signals (version number, last-updated date, author)
- Ability to confirm document currency without asking a colleague

**Success criteria:**
- Participant reaches the correct document in ≤ 3 clicks
- Time on task ≤ 90 seconds
- Participant can state whether the document is current (pass/fail)

**Related hypothesis:** H4 — Centralised Knowledge Hub cuts information search time

---

## Task 2 — Find Who to Contact About a Specific Problem

**Scenario given to participant:**
> "You are dealing with a technical issue with your work laptop and you're not sure who handles IT support in the company. Using the dashboard, find out who to contact and how to reach them."

**What we are testing:**
- Dashboard home layout — is the team contacts section surfaced and discoverable?
- Visual hierarchy — can users find people/contacts without a search?
- Label clarity — is "IT Support" or the contact section labelled in a way users understand?

**Success criteria:**
- Participant identifies the correct contact in ≤ 2 minutes
- No more than 1 wrong navigation step
- Participant can state the contact's name and preferred channel (email/Teams)

**Related hypothesis:** H1 — Unified dashboard reduces cognitive load; H5 — Clear visual navigation

---

## Task 3 — Use the AI Assistant and Understand Its Answer

**Scenario given to participant:**
> "You want to find out what the company's expense reimbursement limit is for client meals. Ask the AI assistant this question and tell us whether you would trust and act on its answer."

**What we are testing:**
- Discoverability of the AI Assistant from the dashboard
- Clarity of the AI's response
- Presence and usefulness of source transparency (source document name, last-updated date, confidence indicator)
- Whether the participant feels they could act on the answer without double-checking with a colleague

**Success criteria:**
- Participant locates the AI assistant in ≤ 2 clicks
- Participant can identify the source of the AI answer within 30 seconds
- Post-task trust rating ≥ 4/5
- Participant says (unprompted or prompted) whether they would act on the answer

**Related hypothesis:** H3 — Source transparency builds AI trust

## Test Script Notes

### Pre-task introduction (read aloud):
> "We are going to show you a prototype of a workplace platform. This is not a real product — it is a design prototype. Nothing you click will break anything. We want to learn from your experience, not test your skills. Please think aloud as you work through each task — tell us what you're looking at, what you're thinking, and what you expect to happen. There are no wrong answers."

### Post-task questions (after all 3 tasks):
1. On a scale of 1–5, how easy was it to complete these tasks? (SUS follows)
2. On a scale of 1–5, how confident would you feel using this platform in a real job?
3. Was there anything confusing or missing?
4. What was the most helpful part of the dashboard?
5. Would you trust the AI assistant's answers in a real workplace setting? Why?
